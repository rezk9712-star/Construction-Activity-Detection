AECO Governance Checklist
1. Data Provenance
Source: Roboflow Universe Public AECO Datasets and localized site imagery.

Date Collection: 2024–2026.

Owner: Educational/Open Source research license.

2. PII Handling (Privacy)
Faces/License Plates: Present in background site imagery but minimized.

Protection Strategy: Faces and sensitive identifiers were blurred using Roboflow Augmentations to ensure worker anonymity.

3. Risk Statement
High-Impact False Negative: Missed Activity/Equipment. Failing to detect active machinery can lead to inaccurate project scheduling or dangerous site-entry errors.

High-Impact False Positive: Nuisance Alerts. Mistaking a dirt mound for an excavator (Class Confusion) leads to "alert fatigue," causing staff to ignore the system's output.

4. Human-in-the-Loop
Review Process: This model is for screening and activity logging only. A Project Engineer or Site Manager must verify all automated logs before they are used for official progress reporting.

Type: MIT / Open Source for Educational Use.

🏗️ Construction Activity Governance & Ethics Checklist

1. Site Operational Privacy
Activity-Only Monitoring: The model is strictly designed to detect machinery and equipment activity; it is not intended for the surveillance of individual worker productivity or biometric tracking.

Data Anonymization: To comply with site privacy standards, any high-resolution imagery containing legible faces or personal identifiers was blurred during the dataset preparation phase.

2. Model Reliability in Construction Contexts
Environmental Variance: Users are cautioned that detection accuracy may drop in common construction environments such as heavy dust, shadows from scaffolding, or during extreme weather conditions.

Class Distinction: The model is trained to distinguish between active machinery (e.g., moving excavators) and static site elements. However, visual similarities in "Construction Orange" materials can lead to false positives.

3. Operational Guardrails
Non-Critical Decision Making: This tool is an operational aid for project tracking and activity logging. It must not be the sole automated source for high-risk safety shutdowns or heavy machinery movement control.

Human-in-the-Loop: All automated activity logs generated by this YOLOv8 model should be reviewed by a Site Manager or Project Engineer to verify accuracy before being used in official project reports.

4. Bias & Data Representation
Machinery Diversity: We recognize that the model may perform better on common yellow or orange machinery (like CAT or JCB equipment) and may require further training for less common colors or specialized AECO tools.

⚠️ Activity Detection Error Governance

1. False Positive (FP) Risks & Mitigations
Misinterpreting site terrain or construction materials as active machinery may create inaccurate activity records.

Mitigation:
The model may experience class confusion, particularly between earth mounds, excavation areas, and construction equipment.
Users are advised to verify automated detections against site photos before making operational decisions.

2. False Negative (FN) Risks & Mitigations
Risk (Data Under-Reporting): Failur to detect construction activities,espicially small or partially visible equipment or materials may result in incomplete activity records.

To address scale variation and occlusion, the dataset improvement plan includes Mosaic Augmentation and additional diverse training images.

In its current state, the model should be treated as a support tool for activity monitoring rather than a fully automated reporting system.

3. Ethical Use of Detection Data
Data Bias: Detection results should be used as decision-support information, not as fully automated construction reporting.
Automated activity detections should be verified against site images before operational decisions are made

Accuracy Transparency: All project stakeholders must be informed of the model's baseline mAP score to understand the inherent margin of error in automated construction reporting.
